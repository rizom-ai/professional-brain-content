AI does not just automate work.
It exposes what an organization has been leaving unsaid.

A lot of institutional life depends on tacit knowledge:
who actually knows the history of a decision,
which exception matters,
where authority really sits,
when a process should be followed,
and when it has to be repaired.

For a long time, that layer could stay informal because people carried it.
Not perfectly.
Not fairly.
But enough to keep things moving.

AI changes the bargain.

The moment a system is allowed to summarize, recommend, classify, prioritize, or act, context stops being optional.
Roles need to be clearer.
Responsibility needs to be traceable.
Exceptions can no longer live only as folklore.

That doesn’t mean everything should become rigid or bureaucratic.
It means automation without context doesn’t remove ambiguity.
It relocates it into the system.

I wrote more about this here:
https://yeehaa.io/essays/a-colleague-without-context

The organizations that use AI well will not be the ones that automate the fastest.
They will be the ones that know what must remain situated.

What parts of your organization still depend on tacit knowledge?
