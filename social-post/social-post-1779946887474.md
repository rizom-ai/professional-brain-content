Ecosystems are not networks.

A network is just the visible part.

The real ecosystem is what happens underneath: the trust people rely on, the memory they inherit, the governance they usually only notice when it breaks.

That is why mapping connections is never enough.

You can have all the right nodes and still have a system that forgets, hesitates, duplicates work, avoids conflict, or depends on a few heroic people to hold everything together.

The architecture is not in the diagram.

It is in what the system remembers, who it trusts, and how it decides.

That is what makes collaboration survive over time.
