We've been telling people to find work that matters.

But we haven't changed the structures that make meaningful work nearly impossible.

Remote work solved geography. AI is solving productivity. But neither solves the real problem: we're still running living systems like machines.

The org chart hasn't changed in a century. We just made it distributed.

What if we designed organizations the way ecosystems work? Not top-down control, but conditions for emergence. Not extracting value from people, but circulating it between them.

This is what I've been building toward with Rizom: a network where independent professionals and AI agents work side by side, where talent flows to where it's needed most, and where ownership is shared by everyone who contributes.

The future of work isn't remote. It isn't AI-powered. It's play.

Not play as in frivolous. Play as in creative, voluntary, meaningful. The kind of work you'd choose even if you didn't have to.

We're building that future now.
