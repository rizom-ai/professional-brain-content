Your team used to move fast. Then you hit 15 people and suddenly everything slowed down.

The problem isn't incompetence. It's that your tools and structures haven't evolved with your team.

Most founders treat people and technology as separate problems. Hire better people. Buy better software. But scaling isn't about upgrading inputs—it's about designing how they work together.

I've seen this play out dozens of times. A startup that shipped in weeks now takes months to align on decisions. Knowledge lives in Slack threads and Notion docs no one can find. Your best engineers are context-switching between code, meetings, and documentation instead of building.

The issue? You're still operating with structures built for a 5-person team.

Ecosystem architecture is about designing your organization so people and technology actually amplify each other. Not as separate layers, but as an integrated system.

This isn't consulting theater. It's a practical diagnosis of what's actually slowing you down.
