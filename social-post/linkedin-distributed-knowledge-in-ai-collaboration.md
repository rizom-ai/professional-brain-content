Most AI implementations fail at the team level.

Not because the models aren't good enough. But because nobody has mapped who knows what—and who trusts whom to know it.

This is a Transactive Memory System problem.

TMS is a deceptively simple idea: effective teams don't need everyone to know everything. They need to know who knows what, and trust that system implicitly. When you hand a problem to a teammate, you're not checking their brain—you're trusting the directory.

Now add AI.

The moment you integrate an AI system into a team, that directory breaks. Engineers don't know what the model actually understands. Product teams don't trust the AI's judgment on tradeoffs. Nobody knows where the boundaries are.

The result? Friction, redundant work, bottlenecks, and teams that revert to treating AI as a tool instead of a collaborator.

The fix isn't better AI. It's making the transactive memory system explicit.

You need clarity on: What does the AI actually know? Where does human judgment still matter? How do we trust this hybrid system?
