Most founders hit the same wall around 15-30 people.

Your scrappy, move-fast culture worked when everyone fit in a room. But now your engineers and operations teams are speaking different languages. Product decisions that used to take a conversation now require three meetings.

The problem isn't that you've grown. It's that your *structures* haven't evolved with you.

This is where ecosystem architecture comes in—designing your organization as a living system, not a machine. It's about creating conditions where technical and human collaboration naturally flourish, even as you scale.

I've watched this play out across dozens of teams. The ones that thrive aren't necessarily the smartest or fastest. They're the ones who deliberately redesigned how knowledge flows, how decisions get made, and how teams actually work together.
