Most teams don't have a knowledge system.

They have a knowledge habit.

Years of intuition passing as a system. Someone knows where the thing is. Someone else remembers why we decided that. The new person learns by osmosis or gets lost.

Then you add AI.

And suddenly the whole thing breaks.

Not because AI is bad. Because AI exposes what was never a real system to begin with.

Here's the thing though: that's actually a gift.

When AI forces you to articulate what you know, how you know it, and who knows what—you get to build what you should have had all along. A real knowledge architecture. Not for the machine. For the humans who actually do the work.

I've been thinking about this a lot. About transactive memory. About how teams actually learn. About what changes when you design for both human and machine cognition.

I'm considering building a workshop around it: "Who Knows What? Designing Hybrid Human-AI Teams That Actually Work."

Before I commit, I want to know: does this land?

Is this something your team is wrestling with?

Comment "WORKSHOP" if you want this to exist.

Or "WE ARE THE BORG" if you think resistance is futile and teams don't need to be redesigned.
