What happens when you stop treating AI as a tool to replace human judgment and start designing for genuine collaboration?

I've been exploring this with a project called "bots with an attitude"—agents that don't just follow orders, but participate in the thinking.

The difference is subtle but profound. Most AI integration assumes humans stay in control. But real collaboration means agents can push back, suggest alternatives, even disagree.

We're living in an interesting moment. We have the technical capability to build systems that work *with* us, not just *for* us. The question isn't whether they can—it's whether we have the organizational and cultural maturity to design for it.

I'm working through what this actually looks like in practice. More on this soon.
