What if the most advanced model of decentralization isn’t blockchain at all?

It might be Shenzhen.

In my latest essay, I look at the shanzhai ecosystem and the practice of gongkai — open sharing of hardware designs — as a living example of decentralized production at scale.

What makes it fascinating is not just the speed of iteration.
It’s the way knowledge, parts, and capabilities move through the network without waiting for permission.

That said, the model is not romantic.
The same dynamics that make it resilient and adaptive also produce serious environmental costs.
That tension matters, because it forces us to ask a harder question:

What does decentralized innovation look like when we include ecological responsibility in the design?

I think this is one of the most important questions for anyone working on future organizational and technical systems.
