We talk about innovation as if it belongs to the future: new ideas, new technologies, new markets, new worlds.

But innovation often begins when the present stops looking inevitable. When “the way things are” becomes visible as something built: maintained by habits, incentives, tools, roles, and relations.

And if it was built, it can be rebuilt.

Not always through disruption, moonshots, or adding more technology. Sometimes innovation is simply looking again.

Innovation is not the future arriving. It is the present losing its inevitability.
