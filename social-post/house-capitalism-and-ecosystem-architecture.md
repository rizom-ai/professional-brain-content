What if the problem isn’t scale, but the metaphor we keep using?

We talk about organizations like houses, machines, and towers.
Stable. Controlled. Designed from the top down.

But healthy ecosystems work differently.
They are regenerative.
They are interconnected.
They support life by circulating value instead of concentrating it.

Extractive systems do the opposite.
They pull resources upward.
They enrich the center while weakening the foundations.
And over time, that makes the whole structure brittle.

This is why I keep coming back to ecosystem architecture.
Not as a buzzword.
As a design question.

If we want organizations that can adapt, learn, and remain alive under pressure, we need structures that behave more like living systems than closed containers.
