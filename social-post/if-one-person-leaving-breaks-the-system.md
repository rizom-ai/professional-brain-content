If one person leaving would make the system wobble, you do not have a people problem.

You have an infrastructure problem.

Every organization has people who “just know” how things work.

Who to ask.
Which process matters.
Which exception never got documented.
Where the real decision happens.
How to translate between teams that no longer understand each other.

It is tempting to call those people invaluable.
Often, they are.

But indispensability is also a diagnostic.

It tells you that memory is not circulating.
That coordination depends on private context.
That judgment has not been made shareable.
That the institution is borrowing capacity from individuals instead of carrying it itself.

The goal is not to make people replaceable in the cold, industrial sense.

The goal is to make knowledge, trust, and coordination less trapped.

Good institutions still need exceptional people.
They just should not require them to compensate for broken form every day.

That is the idea behind my latest essay, *Heroics Are Not Infrastructure*:
https://yeehaa.io/essays/heroics-are-not-infrastructure

Where does your organization still depend too much on “the person who knows”?
