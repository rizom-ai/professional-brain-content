Decentralization didn’t start with crypto.

It didn’t even start with open source.

One of the most interesting models I’ve come across is Shenzhen’s shanzhai ecosystem — where hardware is built through gongkai: open sharing of designs, parts, and production knowledge.

What makes this fascinating is not just the speed of iteration.
It’s the way value is produced across a network of makers, suppliers, and improvisers.

That model feels closer to a living system than a traditional company.
Ideas move quickly.
Components get recombined.
Innovation emerges from proximity, exchange, and adaptation.

But there’s a shadow side too.
The same pace that makes shanzhai so powerful also generates waste at scale.
That tension matters, because any serious conversation about decentralized innovation has to include the environmental cost.

I wrote more about this in Shenzhen Goes Ham:
https://yeehaa.io/essays/shenzhen-goes-ham

If we want to build better decentralized systems, maybe the question is not just how fast they can grow — but what they leave behind.

What decentralized model do you think is most underrated today?
