We're building something that shouldn't work but does.

Bots with an attitude.

Not the kind that execute commands. The kind that push back. That have opinions. That make you think differently about what you're building.

For years, we've treated AI as a tool—something that does what you tell it to do, faster and cheaper. But what if the most interesting collaborations happen when humans and agents actually disagree? When the bot says "that won't work" and means it?

I've been exploring this at the intersection of ecosystem design and human-agent collaboration. How do we build systems where intelligence—human and otherwise—creates something neither could alone?

There's a lot to unpack here. New frameworks. Real examples. Uncomfortable questions about what we're actually optimizing for.

More coming soon.
