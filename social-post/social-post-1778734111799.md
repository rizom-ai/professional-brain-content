AI is a stress test for your organization.

Not because it creates new problems.

Because it reveals the ones you were already carrying.

Where does knowledge actually live?  
How much context is shared?  
What breaks when key people step away?  
How much of the system depends on memory, workarounds, and informal translation?

For years, many organizations could hide those weaknesses inside meetings, proximity, and heroic employees.

AI makes that harder.

The moment you try to extend intelligence across a system, you find out whether there is anything durable to extend:

shared memory,  
clear structure,  
reusable context,  
judgment others can build on.

If those things are missing, AI does not make the organization smarter.

It makes the gaps visible.

That is part of what I’m building toward with Rizom:  
https://rizom.ai

The question is not only what AI can do for your organization.

It is what AI reveals about how your organization actually works.
