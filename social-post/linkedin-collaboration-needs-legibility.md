In most organizations, collaboration is still mostly an accident.

We like to believe that if we put good people in the same room, collaboration will happen.
It usually doesn’t.

Talent is not the bottleneck.
Legibility is.

Too much stays invisible:
what people are actually working on,
what they bring,
what they need,
where the overlap is,
and where collaboration could even begin.

That gap matters more than we admit.
Because when work is opaque, people don’t collaborate — they guess.
And guessing is expensive.

I’ve been thinking a lot about this in relation to a deck I recently put together, Good People Are Not Enough.
It’s a short argument for why collaboration doesn’t emerge from goodwill alone.
It has to be designed for.

If you want the fuller version, I put it here:
https://yeehaa.io/presentations/good-people-are-not-enough

The real question is not whether people are capable of working together.
It’s whether the organization makes it possible to see how.

Where have you seen collaboration fail because people couldn’t see each other clearly enough?
