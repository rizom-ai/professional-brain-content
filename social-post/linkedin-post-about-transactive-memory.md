The best teams don’t rely on everyone knowing everything.

They rely on something more powerful:
transactive memory.

That’s the shared understanding of who knows what, who has the context, and where the knowledge lives.

In practice, this changes everything.

A strong team isn’t a group of generalists pretending to be complete.
It’s a network of people who know how to find each other’s expertise quickly and confidently.

That’s why some organizations feel fluid and intelligent,
while others feel slow and repetitive.

The difference is often not individual brilliance.
It’s whether the group has built a usable memory.

For knowledge work, this matters even more.
We spend so much time optimizing documents, tools, and workflows.
But if people don’t know who to ask, what to trust, or where the right insight sits, the system still fails.

This is one reason I keep thinking in ecosystem terms.
Real organizational intelligence is distributed.
It lives in relationships, not just repositories.
