# Trust vs Security Paradox

## Summary
The content explores how overreliance on security measures paradoxically undermines trust in digital and social systems, arguing that building networks of trust is more effective than implementing security-only solutions. It demonstrates through historical examples like spam filtering that evidence-based trust mechanisms outperform algorithmic policing and suspicious-mind approaches.

## Content
The post examines the fundamental paradox that security measures, while intended to create safe spaces, often damage trust relations between digital citizens and organizations. Historically, code was developed to enable secure communication against suspicion, creating a tradeoff where security came at the expense of trust. The author argues that most attempts to create safe digital spaces based purely on security measures failed or backfired. The spam filtering example illustrates this: early blacklist and domain-blocking approaches were ineffective until ISPs shifted to reputation lists and whitelistingâ€”building networks of trusted partners rather than policing offenders. Similarly, anti-virus software followed a comparable trajectory. The core insight is that technology alone cannot solve trust problems; instead, coalitions of actual people and organizations are essential. The solution requires evidence-based approaches to safety rather than purely algorithmic or anecdotal trust. Public Badges represent an attempt to enable organizations to communicate their values and trustworthiness through evidence rather than anecdotes, creating sustainable safety through trust investment rather than security enforcement.
