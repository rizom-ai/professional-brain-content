---
title: Institutional Decay
---
A condition in which an institution retains its formal structure while losing the capacity to coordinate, remember, and make meaning. Decay can appear as hollowed-out procedures, opaque decision-making, fragmented records, and growing reliance on informal channels or exceptional individuals to do essential work. The institution may seem intact or even overfull with documentation, yet its knowledge is dispersed, inaccessible, or no longer composes a usable whole. This topic is useful for analyzing legitimacy, governance, procedural hollowing, and the gap between official process and actual practice in organizations, governments, nonprofits, and large enterprises.
