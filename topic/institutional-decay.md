---
title: Institutional Decay
---
This topic examines how institutions can appear intact while their actual authority, coordination, and memory have begun to erode. Formal elements like titles, meetings, and procedures continue to exist, but they no longer carry the real work of the institution. Instead, the structure becomes hollow and increasingly dependent on informal workarounds. The post frames this as a gradual failure of form rather than an abrupt collapse. It suggests that institutional weakness often becomes visible only when exceptional people are required to keep everything functioning.
