Ecosystem architecture is presented as a way of designing organizations, communities, and digital spaces as living systems rather than machines. The content repeatedly contrasts this approach with hierarchical, industrial, and extractive models, arguing that contemporary tools and contexts have outgrown them. It emphasizes decentralization, regeneration, and alignment as core principles for creating systems that can adapt over time. The topic also frames ecosystems as something that must be intentionally cultivated, not assumed to emerge on their own. Across the material, ecosystem architecture becomes the umbrella concept tying together knowledge infrastructure, distributed collaboration, and resilient community design.
