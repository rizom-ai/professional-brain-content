{"title":"Ecosystem Architecture","content":"Ecosystem architecture is the practice of designing and cultivating organizations, communities, and digital spaces as living systems rather than machines. It combines decentralization, distributed collaboration, regenerative design, and simple generative rules to create adaptive structures that can emerge, evolve, and renew themselves. Rather than relying on fixed blueprints or centralized control, it treats innovation as an ongoing cycle of alignment, experimentation, validation, adaptation, and gradual distribution of responsibility. Regeneration goes beyond sustainability: it seeks to transform fragmentation, waste, and breakdown into sources of future possibility. The practice aims to create resilient, equitable, and inhabitable futures shaped by shared values, while drawing on traditions such as decentralized organizations, DAOs, mesh networks, Afrofuturism, Shanzhai culture, and cellular automata. Its goal is to help people and organizations create, connect, and share knowledge through structures capable of continuous renewal.","keywords":["organizational design","intentional communities","digital ecosystems","decentralized organizations","distributed collaboration","emergence","regenerative design","adaptive systems","resilience","knowledge infrastructure"]}
