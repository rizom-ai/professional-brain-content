{"content":"Ecosystem architecture is the practice of designing and cultivating organizations and intentional communities as living systems rather than mechanical structures. It addresses the limits of top-down institutions in contexts shaped by distributed tools, decentralized participation, and complex social relationships. Instead of imposing fixed blueprints, it establishes conditions in which simple principles, shared values, and community practices can generate adaptive and unexpected forms of collective life. Its process is iterative: align participants around a moral challenge and future vision, validate genuine commitment through practice, gradually scale capabilities into the community, and enable the ecosystem to become self-sustaining. The approach draws on decentralized organizations, mesh networks, regenerative thinking, Afrofuturism, Shanzhai culture, philosophy of technology, cellular automata, and Michel de Certeau’s concept of sieve-order. It does not reject hierarchy outright; it seeks structures capable of adaptation, participation, emergence, and continuous repair. Rizom applies this practice through knowledge infrastructure that helps people and organizations create, connect, and share knowledge, with the broader aim of building inhabitable institutions that can thrive beyond the control of their initiators.","keywords":["ecosystem architecture","living systems","intentional communities","decentralized organizations","bottom-up emergence","regenerative design","community governance","knowledge infrastructure","organizational design","institutional development"]}
