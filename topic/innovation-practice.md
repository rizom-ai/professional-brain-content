---
title: Innovation Practice
---
Innovation practice is framed as something ongoing and procedural rather than a property of a finished artifact. The content argues that real innovation comes from continuous experimentation, refinement, and adaptation inside communities and organizations. This shifts attention away from the spectacle of breakthrough products and toward the conditions that make useful change possible. It is closely linked to decentralization and regeneration, where innovation should not depend on extraction, excess, or waste. The text also suggests that good practices can generate better futures by altering the rules within which creativity happens. This makes innovation a social and organizational discipline, not just a technical outcome.
