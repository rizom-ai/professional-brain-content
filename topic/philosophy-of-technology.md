A major theme in the profile is the role of philosophy in shaping technological practice. The author describes coming to technology through continental philosophy, particularly through thinkers like Heidegger, Bataille, Benjamin, and Kittler. This philosophical background reframed the questions they wanted to pursue, shifting attention toward knowledge, community, and meaning in digital infrastructure. The text suggests that important cultural and ethical questions are increasingly being answered by the people building technology rather than by academia alone. This gives technology a political and cultural dimension, not just a technical one. The result is a practice grounded in reflection, critique, and intentional design.
