---
title: Internet Fragmentation
---
Internet fragmentation is explored as a structural condition that can produce multiplicity, diversity, and new forms of digital community. Rather than treating fragmented online spaces only as broken pieces of a lost universal whole, the foam metaphor frames them as interconnected bubbles that are self-contained yet mutually dependent. Filter bubbles can provide belonging, solidarity, and shared values, making them potential ecosystems rather than problems that should always be eliminated. At the same time, these spaces create risks of entrapment when people cannot leave or do not belong, and solipsism when a community assumes its own perspective is universal. The challenge is to address these harms without imposing a single worldview or returning to universal norms. This perspective encourages the cultivation of plural, interconnected ecosystems with greater awareness of their boundaries and limitations. Relevant keywords include internet fragmentation, filter bubbles, digital ecosystems, multiplicity, online communities, foam metaphor, pluralism, echo chambers, entrapment, solipsism.
