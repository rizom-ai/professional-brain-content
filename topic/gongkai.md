A major topic in the article is gongkai, the open sharing of hardware designs, blueprints, and bills of materials within the Shenzhen ecosystem. The text argues that gongkai creates a more effective model for open hardware than traditional Western open source frameworks. By making design information widely available, it reduces barriers for hardware hackers and enables faster iteration across the supply chain. The article presents gongkai as a networked approach to intellectual property, where value is created through sharing, reuse, and mutual dependence. This practice is described as central to the speed and adaptability of Shenzhen’s hardware culture. It is framed as a distinctive form of openness shaped by local production realities rather than software-centric ideals.
