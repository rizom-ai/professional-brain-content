---
title: Ecosystem Resilience
---
Ecosystem resilience appears throughout the material as a key reason to prefer distributed, adaptive systems. Whether discussing natural ecosystems, digital communities, or manufacturing networks, the text emphasizes the ability to survive shocks, adapt to change, and keep functioning without a central authority. Resilience is linked to diversity, decentralization, and the freedom for members to move or reorganize when conditions change. It also includes awareness of the shadow side of resilience, such as waste, fragmentation, and environmental cost. In this sense, resilience is not framed as static robustness but as ongoing responsiveness. The topic ties together many of the work’s ecological and organizational arguments.
