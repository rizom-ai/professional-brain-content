# Moonshot Culture Critique

## Summary
A critical examination of the moonshot factory model popularized by Google X, analyzing how the focus on 10x exponential impact and massive resource allocation has become a marketing trend without delivering proportional real-world solutions. The piece contrasts this approach with practical, low-impact problem-solving that addresses actual user needs.

## Content
Google X, led by Astro Teller, introduced the moonshot factory concept based on the idea of investing in problems that could deliver 10x impact over the status quo. The model follows three steps: focus on an unsolvable problem, spend massive resources on it, and sell the solution. Despite a decade of effort and numerous high-profile projects like Google Glass and Waymo, the actual impact on world's biggest problems remains minimal. The moonshot approach has inspired numerous copycats across agencies, incubators, and VC firms that have adopted the marketing language while modifying the formula to focus on fashionable problems rather than truly unsolvable ones. These imitators ask for outrageous amounts of money while promising to cure cancer, end poverty, and solve world hunger. The author argues this represents a disconnect between the aspirational rhetoric and actual outcomes, noting that many entrepreneurs now seek investors to fund their ambitions rather than solve specific problems. The moonshot culture has become more about ego, glory, and impressive Wired covers than delivering meaningful solutions.
