---
title: Social Contracts
---
The shared framework of values, obligations, and expectations that gives political institutions legitimacy and makes formal rules effective in practice. A social contract sustains civic trust, constitutional norms, and state authority by aligning public recognition with institutional order. When that underlying agreement weakens, institutions become easier to bypass, selectively obey, or suspend under pressure. Because it is plural and ongoing, a social contract must be renewed through debate, negotiation, and collective commitment to support democracy, governance, and institutional resilience over time. This topic is useful for discussing political legitimacy, civic stability, constitutional order, and the conditions under which institutions remain effective in periods of stress or polarization.

Keywords: social contract, political legitimacy, civic trust, constitutional norms, state legitimacy, institutional resilience, collective agreement, public recognition, democracy, governance
