title: Social Contracts
---
A social contract is the shared set of values, obligations, and expectations that gives legitimacy to political institutions and makes formal rules meaningful. Constitutions and state structures depend on continued public recognition; when that underlying agreement weakens, institutions become easier to bypass, selectively obey, or suspend in practice. Crises such as COVID can expose how fragile those norms really are, revealing that institutional failure often reflects a broken collective pact rather than isolated abuse. The central political task is therefore to renew shared commitments and rebuild the common basis on which institutions rest, rather than focusing only on repairing visible structures alone.
