---
title: Social Contracts
---
The post argues that the real foundation of a healthy state is not its formal institutions but the social contract beneath them. Institutions are portrayed as legitimate only when they reflect shared values that citizens continue to recognize and uphold. When those values fragment, institutions lose authority and become easy to ignore or selectively obey. The article treats the constitution as insufficient on its own, describing it more as an agreed fiction than a reliable safeguard. It suggests that rebuilding political life requires renewing the underlying agreements that make institutions meaningful. This makes social contract formation the central political problem the series intends to address.
