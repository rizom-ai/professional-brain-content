---
title: Institutional Reform
---
A major theme is the claim that existing public institutions cannot simply be repaired through conventional reform. The author argues that many institutions were designed for industrial-age conditions and no longer fit contemporary social and technological realities. Rather than defending institutions as they are, the article calls for reconsidering the assumptions that shaped them. It frames institutional failure as widespread and bipartisan, not limited to one political side. The post uses examples such as the pandemic and international noncompliance to show how quickly institutional legitimacy breaks down. The proposed response is not preservation for its own sake, but redesign grounded in newer forms of civic organization.
