# Ecosystem Architecture and Future Design

## Summary
The article proposes ecosystem architecture as a practice for designing and cultivating multiple inhabitable futures based on shared values and lived needs. Rather than accepting predetermined outcomes, this approach emphasizes intentional design of alternative social and technological systems that can peacefully coexist.

## Content
Ecosystem architecture is presented as the antidote to surveillance capitalism and predetermined futures. The practice involves investing in futures we would like to inhabit rather than settling for the one we expect to become reality. The decentralized internet's true promise was the multiplication of public and private spaces with unprecedented ownership, but this was lost when responsibility was outsourced to governments and corporations. The article argues that while the internet is now fragmented, it is still possible to prevent further drift apart through swift and clear interventions. Ecosystem architecture requires first aligning the misaligned before connecting the unconnected. Alignment is not about asking people what they want, but rather understanding what people need, shaping tangible futures from these needs, and presenting multiple viable alternatives for people to choose between. The practice draws inspiration from Afrofuturism and counter-futures that create temporal complications and reject linear progress narratives.
