title: Institutional Memory
---
Institutional memory is the capacity of an organization to preserve and transmit the knowledge needed for action across time. It is not the same as storage: archives, documents, dashboards, and chat logs may preserve traces of activity without preserving the meaning, context, and judgment required to use them well. Strong institutional memory makes the past available to the present in a useful way, helping people understand exceptions, procedures, precedents, and hidden dependencies.

When memory is distributed across documentation, systems, and shared practices, organizations become more legible, resilient, and less dependent on a few long-serving insiders. When it lives mainly in people’s heads, the institution becomes fragile under turnover and harder to coordinate. But preservation alone is not enough; too much accumulation can create opacity instead of clarity.
