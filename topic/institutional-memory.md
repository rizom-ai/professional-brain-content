---
title: Institutional Memory
---
Institutional memory is the capacity of an organization to preserve and transmit critical knowledge, context, exceptions, and procedures across time. When that memory lives in archives, documentation, and shared systems, the institution can coordinate reliably and survive turnover. When it migrates into the heads of veterans and fixers, the organization becomes person-dependent, fragile, and harder to scale. Strong institutions distribute memory across structures so they can remain legible, resilient, and less dependent on the exhausted few who remember how things really work. This makes memory part of the deeper infrastructure of legitimacy and coordination, not just a record of the past, but a condition for ongoing institutional life.

Keywords: archival systems, documentation, knowledge retention, organizational continuity, shared procedures, exceptions management, staff turnover, organizational resilience, coordination, legitimacy
