A major thread is the design of knowledge infrastructure for teams and organizations. The content repeatedly returns to the problem of scattered tools, hidden expertise, onboarding friction, and the lack of shared memory in distributed groups. Solutions such as Rizom Brains, Lefthoek, and earlier work on Offcourse all focus on making knowledge more legible, portable, and actionable. This infrastructure is not just about storing information; it is about helping people find who knows what, preserve context, and reduce coordination costs. The emphasis is on systems that support collaboration without centralizing control. Knowledge becomes something that flows through an ecosystem rather than something locked in repositories or people’s heads.
