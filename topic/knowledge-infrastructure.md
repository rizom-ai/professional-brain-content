title: Knowledge Infrastructure
---
Knowledge infrastructure is the systems layer that enables an organization to capture, preserve, organize, and activate what it knows. It treats organizations as knowledge systems: their ability to think, coordinate, and adapt depends on whether expertise, context, and memory are made shared, legible, and durable across people, time, and distance. Strong knowledge infrastructure reduces dependence on heroics, undocumented habits, and hidden experts by turning operational memory into something portable and resilient. It supports onboarding, coordination, collective memory, distributed work, and trust by making it easier to see who knows what and move knowledge where it is needed. Open standards, readable systems, and intelligent tools can strengthen this layer, especially as AI lowers the cost of execution but raises the importance of legibility and coordination.
