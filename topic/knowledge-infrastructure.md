---
title: Knowledge Infrastructure
---
Knowledge infrastructure is the system an organization uses to capture, preserve, organize, and activate what it knows. It makes expertise legible, shared, and durable across people, time, and distance, so decisions, research, and operational memory do not remain trapped in individuals or scattered tools. Strong knowledge infrastructure supports onboarding, coordination, message synchronization, collective memory, and resilience in distributed work. It often relies on readable, portable systems, open standards, and intelligent tools that help teams identify who knows what and move knowledge where it is needed. The goal is to make organizations think, remember, and act more coherently without centralizing control.
