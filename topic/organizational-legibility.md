Organizational legibility refers to the clarity with which the capacities, needs, and activities of individuals within a collective are made visible to others. In the context of Rizom, the primary challenge is not a lack of talent, but the difficulty in discerning what each person offers, seeks, or is currently working on. This invisibility leads to missed opportunities for collaboration and an overreliance on chance encounters. By prioritizing legibility, Rizom aims to make it easier for members to find points of connection and act on potential collaborations. Tools like Rizom Brains are proposed to systematically surface and organize this information, making the collective more knowable to itself. Enhanced legibility is positioned as a prerequisite for effective coordination and the emergence of genuine collaboration within the collective.
