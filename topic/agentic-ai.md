---
title: Agentic AI
---
Agentic AI describes AI systems that participate actively in shared work rather than acting only as passive tools or standalone replacements. The focus is on meaningful agency: agents can hold specialized knowledge, coordinate tasks, contribute to workflows, and support collaboration within human-led systems. This perspective frames AI in terms of protocols, trust, contribution, and governance, not just command execution or imitation. It is especially relevant to knowledge work, where humans and agents may operate side by side in durable ecosystems that depend on accountability, credibility, and distributed coordination. Rather than treating intelligence as the only metric, agentic AI emphasizes participation in collaborative systems and the organizational design needed to make that participation effective and trustworthy. The topic also connects to transactive memory, where agents function as reliable carriers of knowledge and specialization across a network of people and tools beyond duplicate/near-duplicate keywords.
