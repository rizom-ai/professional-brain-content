---
title: Online Learning
---
Online learning is an important topic in the early work, especially through Offcourse and the reflections on educational technology. The content argues that online education should not be treated as a weaker substitute for in-person learning, but as a distinct medium with its own strengths. It highlights asynchronous access, interactive feedback, curated resources, and the possibility of collaborative knowledge-building across distance. The posts also critique institutional education for its rigidity and for underusing the affordances of digital tools. Learning is framed as something that can be more participatory, self-directed, and community-driven when designed well. This topic connects education to broader questions of infrastructure and autonomy.
