---
title: Online Learning
---
Online learning is presented as a mode of education with its own strengths rather than a deficient substitute for classroom teaching. The discussion highlights short, focused lessons, browser-based practice, automated feedback, hints, and playful course design as ways to create experiences that are difficult to reproduce offline. Code School serves as a model for combining technical interactivity with thematic engagement. Automation is framed as a support for learning rather than a replacement for educators. The topic also includes the challenge of communicating the promise of educational innovation persuasively to institutions. Overall, online education is treated as an opportunity to improve the quality, accessibility, and engagement of higher education.
