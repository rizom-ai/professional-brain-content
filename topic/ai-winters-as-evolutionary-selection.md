# AI Winters as Evolutionary Selection

## Content
Historical AI winters (1974-1980 and 1987-1993) are reframed not as failures but as selection events that systematically chose control over emergence. The first winter rejected bottom-up learning in favor of rule-based systems; the second rejected human-like reasoning for statistical scaling. This pattern reveals how technological development has been shaped by preferences for centralized control rather than distributed intelligence.
