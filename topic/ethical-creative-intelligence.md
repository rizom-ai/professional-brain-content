Ethical creative intelligence reframes artificial intelligence as a practice of meaningful participation in collaborative systems, rather than as a tool for automation or control. This concept highlights the value of designing intelligent agents that act as equal community members, capable of respecting and questioning community values, contributing creatively, and supporting collective knowledge work. It moves beyond the dichotomy of AI as either a threat or a passive service, insisting that agency and ethical alignment matter more than humanness. Through frameworks like 'Bots with an Attitude,' this approach demonstrates how modular, transparent, and participatory AI can augment human collaboration, foster trust, and enable new forms of governance in digital communities.
