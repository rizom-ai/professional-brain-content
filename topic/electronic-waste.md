---
title: Electronic Waste
---
Electronic waste functions as the shadow side of rapid innovation and decentralized production. In the Shenzhen and shanzhai discussions, waste is not treated as an accidental byproduct but as a structural consequence of speed, iteration, and excess. The material connects e-waste to broader critiques of extractive capitalism and the ideology of limitless growth. It also explores whether decentralized systems might be redirected toward more regenerative practices, such as recycling, reuse, and longer product lifespans. This topic ties environmental cost to the moral and material consequences of technological production.
