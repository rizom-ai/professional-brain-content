---
title: Ethical AI
---
The content presents AI as a practical collaborator rather than a generalized intelligence or a threat to human agency. Several projects focus on narrow, specialized agents that handle tedious tasks, support knowledge work, and participate in communities as accountable actors. A recurring idea is that agents should be ethical, creative, and useful within a specific social context rather than abstractly autonomous. The material also explores governance for hybrid human-agent systems, including reputation, trust, and value alignment. Overall, ethical AI is framed as infrastructure for meaningful work, not as a moonshot aimed at replacing people.
