---
title: Surveillance Capitalism
---
Surveillance capitalism is examined as a dominant model in which human experience is extracted and translated into behavioral data. Governments and corporations can use persistent connectivity, large-scale data collection, and predictive analytics to influence or preempt human behavior. The essays challenge the assumption that connecting more people to the internet is automatically beneficial, arguing that access without autonomy can deepen systems of exploitation and control. This critique extends to platform power, political manipulation, misinformation, corporate influence, and the privatization of digital infrastructure. Against predetermined technological futures, the proposed alternative is to reclaim collective responsibility for digital spaces and deliberately design competing futures. Relevant keywords include surveillance capitalism, behavioral data, platform power, data extraction, algorithmic control, privacy, predictive analytics, digital governance, technological determinism, and collective agency.
