Knowledge mapping involves systematically documenting and exposing who knows what within a team or collective. The material points out that in high-functioning teams, success often hinges on the explicit knowledge of members' skills, interests, and ongoing work. In Rizom, the lack of such visibility creates ambiguity and hampers the natural emergence of collaboration. By making knowledge and expertise legible, the collective can more easily identify where collaboration can occur and how to mobilize resources effectively. Tools that facilitate knowledge mapping are thus seen as vital infrastructure for any organization seeking to maximize its collaborative potential.
