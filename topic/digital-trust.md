---
title: Digital Trust
---
Digital trust concerns how online communities and public spaces can establish credibility without relying solely on security controls or personal anecdotes. The content argues that security measures often address suspicion without creating genuine trustworthy relationships, and may even weaken those relationships. Reputation systems, positive endorsements, engagement metrics, and networks of trusted partners are presented as more effective than purely punitive blacklists. Public Badges are proposed as evidence-based signals that allow organizations to communicate the values and practices behind their trustworthiness. Trust should therefore be supported by both human coalitions and technological infrastructure rather than delegated entirely to algorithms. The broader goal is to create safer digital public spaces grounded in visible evidence, shared responsibility, and accountable participation. Relevant keywords include digital trust, reputation systems, Public Badges, online communities, evidence-based trust, internet governance, spam filtering, public spaces, algorithmic accountability.
