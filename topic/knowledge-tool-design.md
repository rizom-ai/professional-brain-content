---
title: Knowledge Tool Design
---
Knowledge tool design examines how software infrastructure shapes the production, preservation, retrieval, and exchange of knowledge. Effective tools should support semantic structure, citation management, collaboration, research-teaching integration, contextual preservation, and dependable discovery. Rather than treating software as neutral or merely administrative, this approach recognizes tools as active forces in disciplinary and organizational development. It emphasizes designing systems around the practices of knowledge communities, while validating retrieval mechanisms and strengthening organizational memory, classification, and decision-making.

Keywords: knowledge infrastructure, information retrieval, knowledge preservation, semantic markup, citation management, collaborative tools, organizational memory, knowledge systems, research software, knowledge communities
