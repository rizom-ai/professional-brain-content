# Surveillance Capitalism and Data Control

## Summary
The piece examines how surveillance capitalism weaponizes behavioral data to predict and control human behavior, positioning it as a threat to digital autonomy. It argues that without deliberate intervention through ecosystem architecture, we risk inhabiting a predetermined dystopian future designed by corporations and governments.

## Content
Surveillance capitalism, as defined by Shoshana Zuboff, unilaterally claims human experience as free raw material for translation into behavioral data. Corporations and governments share a vested interest in connecting people to the internet not for accessibility but to gather larger amounts of granular behavioral data. By analyzing vast behavioral patterns, they can predict and preempt people's actions, creating a vicious cycle of exploitation. The danger is particularly acute because naively expanding internet access without addressing underlying power structures only feeds into this system.
