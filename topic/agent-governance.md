---
title: Agent Governance
---
Agent governance is a recurring concern in the pieces about bots, hybrid teams, and AI-assisted collaboration. The content asks how to manage networks that include both humans and machines without reducing agents to passive tools or granting them unchecked autonomy. Reputation scoring, state machines, community rules, and value alignment are all proposed as governance mechanisms. The aim is to keep agentic systems useful, ethical, and compatible with collective goals. This topic matters because the more capable the agents become, the more important it is to make their participation legible and accountable. Governance here is less about command and control than about designing conditions for trustworthy participation.
