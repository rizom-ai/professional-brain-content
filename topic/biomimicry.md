Biomimicry is discussed as the practice of learning from the forms, processes, and strategies of the natural world. The post highlights Janine Benyus and the idea that nature can inspire innovation across design, engineering, and technology. Biomimicry is contrasted with approaches that seek to control or exploit nature, instead treating the natural world as a source of insight and dialogue. This makes biomimicry part of a broader regenerative mindset that looks to living systems for guidance. Its role in the article is to show how nature can shape more intelligent and responsive human systems.
