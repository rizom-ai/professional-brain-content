> Many institutions have years of documents, decisions, and expertise — but most of it is scattered, implicit, or hard to access. As a result, when they add AI, the system can only surface part of the picture. It may summarize what exists, but it cannot explain why decisions were made, what changed, or what the institution actually knows.
>
> That is the real problem: not the tool, but the institution itself.
>
> AI is a stress test for institutional capability. It shows whether an institution has made its memory, authority, and coordination legible enough to work with. Where those foundations are clear, AI can extend them. Where they are weak, AI will expose the gaps — and often make them worse.
>
> The challenge for the next generation of institutions is not just to automate work. It is to build the infrastructure that lets institutions carry their own memory, judgment, and responsibility.
>
> That means creating systems for sovereign data, living memory, situated agents, regenerative coordination, interoperable ecosystems, and verifiable provenance. The goal is not another software layer. It is living infrastructure for institutions that can carry themselves.
