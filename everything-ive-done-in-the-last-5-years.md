## Main arc
Over the last five years, the work has moved from **philosophy and tools** toward **ecosystem architecture**: building systems for learning, coordination, and knowledge-sharing that behave more like **living systems** than rigid machines.

## Core themes
- **Knowledge as infrastructure**: treating learning, memory, and coordination as practical design problems.
- **From institutions to ecosystems**: questioning inherited organizational models and exploring more distributed, adaptive forms.
- **Writing as a research practice**: using essays to clarify ideas before and alongside building.
- **Tool-making with intent**: building products that express a specific theory of how people and communities should work together.

## Notable outputs
### Offcourse
- An early open-source learning platform.
- Reflected a sustained interest in **online learning outside institutional walls**.
- Helped establish the link between education, community, and software design.

### Public Badges
- Work focused on recognition, learning records, and credentialing.
- Extended the education thread into **portable proof of learning** and alternative recognition systems.

### Bots with an Attitude
- Explored more expressive, intentional automation.
- Suggested an interest in **interface behavior, agency, and the social role of tools**.

### Superconnectors
- Investigated relationships, network effects, and connective tissue in communities.
- Reinforced the idea that value often emerges from **linking people and contexts**, not just producing content.

### Rizom Collective
- A step toward a more explicit ecosystem-oriented practice.
- Positioned collaboration, shared knowledge, and organizational design as part of the work itself.

### Rizom Brains
- Continued the knowledge infrastructure direction.
- Emphasized **sense-making, memory, and collective intelligence** as design challenges.

### Website launch and essay collection
- The public site and growing essay archive made the thinking more legible.
- Writing became a way to articulate the transition from experimentation to a coherent body of work.

## Writing as a through-line
- **When All We Have is a Hammer**: an early signal of critical reflection on tools and approaches.
- **Value Driven Development**: made the case for building from values, not just requirements.
- **The Low End Theory**: pushed back on hype and moonshot thinking in favor of grounded work.
- **The Practice of Ecosystem Architecture**: the clearest articulation of the current framework.
- **Hiding in Plain Sight**: continues the institutional and systems-level critique.

## Current synthesis
The work now centers on **Rizom** as a practical expression of these ideas:
- knowledge infrastructure for organizations that behave like living systems
- tools for creating, connecting, and sharing knowledge
- support for distributed collaboration without defaulting to machine-like hierarchy

## Useful summary
The last five years can be read as a progression:
1. **Philosophy informed the questions**
2. **Education tools provided the first experiments**
3. **Network and community projects deepened the systemic lens**
4. **Writing clarified the theory**
5. **Rizom operationalized the vision**
