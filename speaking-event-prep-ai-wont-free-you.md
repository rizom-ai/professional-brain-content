## 6. Talk Abstract (200 words or less)

Most people use AI the way we used to use typewriters: a faster version of what we already did. That is a waste.

AI will not make you more creative, more productive, or more free by default. Used as a tool — a smarter autocomplete, a faster search engine — it just optimizes the same broken workflows. You move quicker, but you go nowhere new.

The real shift happens when you stop treating AI as a tool and start designing it as a collaborator. When your AI agent understands your context, carries your expertise, and works alongside you. A good agent does not replace your judgment, but amplifies it.

Drawing on seven years of building human-AI systems — from ethical AI agents in distributed communities, to narrow AI networks for organizations, to personal knowledge systems that grow with you — this talk explores what it actually takes to make AI work for you. Not the hype. Not the fear. The architecture.

You will walk away with a practical framework for rethinking how you work with AI: one that prioritizes your agency, your knowledge, and your freedom.
