# Have Your Agent Call My Agent
Jan Hein "Yeehaa" Hoogstad
Hub Hoi An, March 16, 2026, 8pm

---

## Who Am I

Professor turned entrepreneur. Philosopher who codes. I'm here because I became obsessed with a question: what if the future of work isn't about humans using smarter tools, but about humans and machines working together as genuine participants?

---

## The Persistent Dream

ELIZA (1966): We thought we'd cracked understanding. We hadn't.

BDI Agents (1990s): Academia dreamed of perfectly rational agents. The dream stayed in papers.

Siri (2011): Agents went mainstream. They disappointed us in real time.

AutoGPT (2023): The autonomous dream reborn, burned bright, fizzled fast.

OpenClaw (2026): Something shifted. Agents that actually live in your world.

But something has shifted.

---

## Bots with an Attitude (2019)

Planet B was a distributed community, async collaboration. We didn't design agents as tools. We designed them as equal community members.

That meant reframing AI entirely. Not as artificial intelligence, but as ethical creative intelligence. A participant, not a servant.

---

## Lefthoek (2020)

We built a network of specialized AI agents for real organizational problems. Not one general-purpose system trying to do everything. Narrow, focused agents beat the fantasy of omniscience every time.

The insight: automate the boring, free humans for meaningful work. Simple. Radical.

---

## Rizom (2024–now)

A decentralized network where independent professionals and AI agents work side by side. Not a platform extracting value. A living ecosystem where talent flows to where it's needed most, and work serves human flourishing.

The question that drives everything: what if agents aren't tools but genuine participants in how we organize work?

---

## Agency Trumps Humanness

What matters isn't whether an agent is human. It's whether it meaningfully contributes.

This was our design principle from the start, not a conclusion we arrived at. It changes everything, agency over anthropomorphism. Governance. Trust. Collaboration. Ownership.

---

## The Tool Trap

We face a false binary. Either we reduce agents to obedient command executors. Or we hand them full autonomy and walk away.

Both miss the point. Collaboration requires participation, not control or delegation. It requires a third way.

---

## Have Your Agent Call My Agent

This isn't a punchline. It's an architecture.

Transactive Memory Systems: effective teams don't share all knowledge. They share awareness of who knows what. Three dimensions: specialization, credibility, coordination.

Agents as carriers of transactive memory. They hold your expertise. They establish trust. They coordinate on your behalf. The call is the protocol that makes distributed collaboration possible.

---

## Rizom, Grounded

Brains at every level: professional, team, collective knowledge. AI agents like Rover, Recall, Ranger hold specialization, build credibility, enable coordination.

Open standards, no vendor lock-in. You own your knowledge. Not a platform that extracts. A living system that circulates.

---

## The Future We're Entering

Agents won't replace us, and we won't just use them. We'll work alongside them as participants in shared systems of knowledge and trust.

The organizations that thrive will be designed as living systems, not machines. The future of work is play.
