Rizom offers a fundamentally different approach to knowledge work tools. Rather than locking thinking into predetermined structures, their platform—built on core principles of being AI-native, plugin-based, and fully user-owned—allows knowledge workers to capture information as plain text, automatically connect related ideas into a growing knowledge graph, and transform captured knowledge into published content across multiple formats. The platform emphasizes zero lock-in through plain text storage, operates via open standards like the Model Context Protocol, and scales seamlessly from individual creators to entire organizations without requiring tool switching.

The technical foundation prioritizes user autonomy: all content lives as portable plain text files, the system remains agnostic to specific AI providers, and communication integrates with existing messaging platforms rather than replacing them. This architecture reflects Yeehaa's broader philosophy of designing tools and organizations as living systems rather than rigid machines, enabling knowledge work that adapts to how users actually think rather than forcing users to adapt to predetermined workflows.
