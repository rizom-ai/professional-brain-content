# CHT Video Script

**Purpose:** application video — "a two-minute video recording of yourself describing why you are a good fit for the position"
**Companion notes:** CHT Cognition Proposal · CHT Spirituality Proposal · CHT Machine Proposal
**Master note:** CHT Fellowship Overview
**Target:** ~295 words ≈ 2:00 at a natural pace (150 wpm). Practice once with a timer; if over, use the marked cuts.

---

## Script (v1)

**[0:00 — Hook + who I am]**
I'm Jan Hein Hoogstad. I've spent my career on both sides of one question: how do people make meaning and judgment together — and what happens when technology gets in the middle?

**[0:15 — The arc: philosopher → builder]**
I came to technology through philosophy. I was an assistant professor at the University of Amsterdam when I realized the most interesting questions about knowledge, community, and meaning were no longer being asked in academia. They were being answered — for better or worse — by the people building our digital infrastructure. So I became one of them.

**[0:40 — What I've seen from inside]**
For the past decade I've built knowledge infrastructure for organizations: open-source learning platforms, credentialing systems for the Dutch public sector, and now Rizom, where I run transactive-memory diagnostics with organizations deploying AI over their internal knowledge. I've watched, from inside the deployments, what happens when institutions hand their remembering and judging to machines. The demos promise capability. What I actually see is subtler: fluent answers where reasons used to be.

**[1:10 — The two proposals, one program]**
My two proposals are one research program. AI and Cognition asks what happens when institutions outsource judgment. My second proposal asks what happens when people outsource the practices that make meaning. [If Machine is chosen instead: "My second, Inside the Machine, shows — with running code — that AI agents can be built to answer for what they do."] Same mechanism: the output survives, the capacity erodes. And it isn't inevitable — it follows from design choices and business models that can be named and changed. That's CHT's frame. It's also ten years of my field notes.

**[1:40 — The demonstration + close]**
And I'll practice what I argue. The research will run in the open, in a public, provenance-tracked notebook built on my own infrastructure, where you can trace every claim to its source. You won't have to take my word for what accountable AI looks like. You can browse it.

**[2:00 — end]**

## Marked cuts (if running over)

1. Cut "— for better or worse —" (saves 3 words / ~1.5s).
2. Cut "The demos promise capability." (saves 4 words / ~2s) — keep "What I actually see…" and it still lands.
3. Compress the decade sentence to: "For the past decade I've built knowledge infrastructure for organizations — most recently Rizom, where I run transactive-memory diagnostics with organizations deploying AI over their internal knowledge." (saves ~10 words / ~4s.)

## Alternate hook (sharper, riskier)

"For ten years I asked how institutions think. Then I started building the software they think with. I'm Jan Hein Hoogstad…" — use if the delivery feels flat; skip if it feels rehearsed.

## Alternate close (spirituality-forward)

"…You can browse it. Because the question underneath both proposals is the one I've carried since philosophy: what do we lose when we get the answer without the practice of reaching it?" — only if there's time; trades the crisp ending for a thematic one. (Pairs with the Spirituality second proposal.)

## Delivery notes

- **Talking head, one-take feel.** No slides, no screen share — the links in the proposals do the demonstrating; the video should show a person worth listening to for six months. Eye contact with the lens, conversational pace, one beat of silence after the hook question.
- **Register is the white paper's:** calm, declarative, zero hype. Resist the founder-pitch cadence — CHT screens against it.
- **Selection criteria this hits:** interdisciplinary mid-career profile (philosopher + builder), insider vantage ("inside the deployments"), CHT frame in their language (design choices, business models, not inevitable), intellectual honesty ("field notes," traceable claims), impact over performance (open notebook as practice).
- **Fact-check status:** all claims verified against CV and anchor profile — UvA assistant professorship, decade of building (Offcourse 2013→), Public Badges = credentialing for Dutch public sector via PublicSpaces, Rizom TMS diagnostics.
- **Dependency:** the "two proposals" paragraph has a bracketed variant for whichever second proposal (Spirituality or Machine) is chosen — pick one before recording.
- **Practical:** record horizontal, quiet room, natural light, look-at-lens; three takes, pick the warmest not the most polished.
