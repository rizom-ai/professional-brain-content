## Script (v2 — Machine pairing)

**[0:00 — Hook + who I am]**
I'm Jan Hein Hoogstad. I've spent my career on both sides of one question: how do people make meaning and judgment together — and what happens when technology gets in the middle?

**[0:15 — The arc: philosopher → builder]**
I came to technology through philosophy. I was an assistant professor at the University of Amsterdam when I realized the most interesting questions about knowledge, community, and meaning were no longer being asked in academia. They were being answered — for better or worse — by the people building our digital infrastructure. So I became one of them.

**[0:40 — What I've seen from inside]**
For the past decade I've built knowledge infrastructure for organizations: open-source learning platforms, credentialing systems for the Dutch public sector, and now Rizom, where I build and deploy AI agents inside organizations. I've watched, from inside the deployments, what happens when institutions hand their remembering, judging, and acting to machines. The demos promise capability. What I actually see is subtler: fluent answers where reasons used to be, and decisions no one can quite account for.

**[1:10 — The two proposals, one program]**
My two proposals are one research program. AI and Cognition asks what happens to institutional memory and judgment when they're outsourced to AI. Inside the Machine asks what happens to responsibility when action is — and it shows, with running open-source code, that agents can be built to answer for what they do. Both come down to the question underneath my white paper: can an institution still stand behind what it does once memory, judgment, and action move into machines? And it isn't inevitable — it follows from design choices and business models that can be named and changed. That's CHT's frame. It's also ten years of my field notes.

**[1:40 — The demonstration + close]**
And I'll practice what I argue. The research will run in the open, in a public, provenance-tracked notebook built on my own infrastructure, where you can trace every claim to its source. You won't have to take my word for what accountable AI looks like. You can browse it.

**[2:00 — end]**
