# Pilot Proposal: A Cohort Brain for the AI Datacampus

*Companion to the deck "A Campus That Remembers" — for the Datacampus coordinator.*

## The proposal

The deck makes the case that the Datacampus needs a living memory layer, and that the natural place to prove it is a startup cohort — a bounded group, a fixed cycle, and knowledge that visibly compounds (or visibly evaporates) from one cohort to the next. We propose to run that proof together: a pilot with one ECE cohort, starting where your programs already run today, so the Datacampus opens with a working memory layer instead of a plan for one.

## What the pilot includes

- **A shared cohort brain** connecting founders, mentors, program staff, session notes, experiments, decisions, and open questions.
- **Personal brain workflows** for founders who want their knowledge to remain portable and under their own control.
- **Situated agents** that work from the cohort's actual context: surfacing prior feedback, connecting questions to people, supporting onboarding of new mentors and partners.
- **An expertise and knowledge-flow map** of the cohort and its surrounding network.
- **Governance and permissions** — who can see what, what stays personal, what becomes program memory — established from day one.

## Scope and rhythm

One cohort cycle. A scoping session before the cohort starts, light-touch setup in the first weeks, and a review at the end of the cycle. Bounded on purpose: small enough to decide quickly, complete enough to judge honestly.

## What success looks like

By the end of the cycle, three things should be demonstrably true:

1. Founders and mentors actively use the brain — retrieval, not just storage.
2. Program knowledge that previously lived in inboxes and individual memory is captured and findable.
3. The next cohort can start from what this one learned — the concrete story you can put in front of Datacampus stakeholders.

## What we ask

- **Your sponsorship**: the pilot runs inside one of your cohorts, framed as part of the program rather than an add-on tool.
- **Access**: to program staff, mentors, and founders for setup and light onboarding.
- **A scoping session**: to fix the cohort, timeline, success criteria, and investment together. We deliberately keep budget out of this document — it depends on cohort size and how much of the setup your team carries versus ours, and we would rather size it honestly in that conversation than anchor it here.
