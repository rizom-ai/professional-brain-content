# Pilot Proposal: A Formation Brain for the AI Datacampus

*Companion to the deck "A Campus That Remembers" — for the Datacampus coordinator.*

## The proposal

The deck argues that the campus needs a living memory layer, and that memory cannot be retrofitted — it has to be running before the campus opens. The place to prove that is the formation itself. We propose a pilot in which the coalition's own coordination work becomes the first shared brain: partners, roles, commitments, decisions and their rationale, studies, consultations, and open questions — contributed by the partner organizations from day one. The campus's first memory becomes the memory of its own creation.

## What the pilot includes

- **A shared formation brain** holding the coalition's partners, roles, commitments, decisions with their rationale, studies, consultations, and open questions.
- **Contribution workflows per partner organization**, so feeding the brain fits how each partner already works.
- **Situated agents** working from the coalition's actual context: onboarding a new partner representative in an afternoon, retrieving why a decision was taken, connecting a question to the partner who can answer it.
- **A capability map of the coalition** — which partner brings what, visible to all.
- **Permissions from day one** — each partner decides what it shares with the coalition and what stays internal.

## Scope and rhythm

A bounded slice of the formation calendar, fixed together in scoping. Light-touch setup in the first weeks, a review at the end. Bounded on purpose: small enough for the partners to decide quickly, complete enough to judge honestly.

## What success looks like

By the end of the pilot, three things should be demonstrably true:

1. Partner representatives actively use the brain — retrieval and reuse, not just deposit.
2. A new representative gets up to speed in days, not months of meetings.
3. Coalition decisions are traceable with their rationale — concrete proof you can show both the partners and your own board and funders.

## What we ask

- **Your sponsorship**: the pilot is framed as part of the formation process, not a tool bolted onto it.
- **Access**: to the working groups and partner representatives for setup and light onboarding.
- **A scoping session**: to fix scope, timeline, success criteria, and how the investment is shared among the participating partners. We deliberately keep budget out of this document — it depends on the number of partners and the scope we choose, and we would rather size it honestly in that conversation than anchor it here.

The output of the scoping session includes a circulate-able pilot proposal you can put in front of the co-funding partners alongside the deck.
