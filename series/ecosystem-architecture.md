# Ecosystem Architecture

## Description
Ecosystem Architecture explores how to design organizations and communities as living systems rather than machines, drawing on philosophy, nature, and decentralized networks to build structures that thrive through alignment rather than control. From surveillance capitalism to Shenzhen's peer-to-peer manufacturing, this series examines the frameworks, practices, and real-world examples that enable us to cultivate regenerative, inhabitable futures. Discover how fragmentation, excess, and emergence can become the foundation for organizations that work with complexity instead of against it.
